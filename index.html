“Influence Factors Analysis on Personal Happiness:
A Machine Learning Approach Using Multidimensional Data"

In this groundbreaking project, we aim to understand the factors influencing happiness across various contexts and situations by studying two key datasets, 'Survey' and 'Personal'. Using clustering techniques, statistical analysis, and supervised machine learning models, we identify similarities among respondents in the 'Survey' dataset and uncover hidden patterns. Additionally, longitudinal data from real participants in the 'Personal' dataset reveal how daily activities impact subjective happiness.

Our project illustrates the surprising connections between actions and emotions, highlighting variations between perceived and actual influences on happiness. Recognized for excellence, this end-to-end project offers a fascinating journey to deepen understanding of human psychology and challenge perceptions of happiness.
